Preamble

Let it be known to all agents and officers of the Order of the Blood Knights operating within or adjacent to the blighted lands of Tirisfal Glades: this directive constitutes a lawful and binding order of the highest martial classification, issued under the authority of the Magisters' Council of Quel'Thalas and ratified by the standing Knight-Lord Convocation.

Intelligence gathered over the preceding months by our embedded operatives has confirmed a disquieting reality: the Scarlet Crusade is not gone from Tirisfal. A scattered yet coordinated remnant — fanatical survivors of the Crusade's long collapse — continues to operate within the glades, conducting raids upon Forsaken settlements, ambushing travelers on the road to the Undercity, and desecrating sites of strategic significance to allied Horde interests. These zealots represent not merely a military inconvenience, but an ideological contagion that cannot be permitted to fester.

Forsaken outriders have reported torch-marked encampments bearing the Crusade's sun-and-sword insignia at three separate sites along the Brightwater Lake ridge. Local undead civilians have been slain and burned — the Scarlet practice of "purification by flame" — indicating organised, not opportunistic, attack patterns.

A prisoner recovered near the Solliden Farmsteads bearing Crusader rank-pins confessed, under persuasion, that their cell receives supply drops from an unnamed inland contact. The prisoner did not survive questioning, but confirmation of resupply indicates external coordination and an enduring command structure — almost certainly Tirisfal-native.

Subject of Primary Interest
Captain Aldric Vachon KOSUnconfirmed KIA
Former Tirisfal Regional Commander, Scarlet Crusade · Presumed Dead, Year 26
Vachon commanded Scarlet operations throughout northern Tirisfal from a fortified farmhouse east of Brill, a posting he held through the Crusade's mid-period before its internal fractures. He was officially declared slain following the Forsaken's sweeping assault on Crusade encampments in Year 26 — his body never recovered. Horde field reports dismissed the absence of remains as consistent with battlefield destruction.

Recent intelligence contradicts this assessment. Three separate operative accounts describe a "scarred commander in red plate, bearing the old Crusade crest" directing Tirisfal cell activity. One description notes a distinctive wound scar across the left jaw — matching Vachon's documented dossier. It is the Order's working assessment that Aldric Vachon survived, went to ground, and has since reconstituted a command cell from the remnants of his former regiment.

Vachon is assessed as extremely dangerous: a veteran field commander, skilled with sword and holy light, deeply familiar with Tirisfal's terrain, and motivated by unchecked zealotry. Capture is not the objective. Vachon is to be eliminated.

Operative Clauses & Standing Mandates
I.
Clause of Engagement — Lethal Authority: All agents acting under this order are hereby granted full lethal authority against any confirmed member of the Scarlet Crusade operating within the boundaries of Tirisfal Glades. No parley shall be offered. No quarter shall be given. The Crusade's ideology constitutes an irredeemable threat; prisoner-taking is expressly not sanctioned unless intelligence value is deemed exceptional by a ranking Knight-Lord present in the field.
II.
Clause of Identification — Rank Insignia: Agents must, where tactically feasible, confirm Crusader affiliation before engagement via visible sun-and-sword iconography, Scarlet tabard, or spoken invocation of Crusade doctrine. Misidentification of Forsaken civilians as Crusaders will be treated as a disciplinary matter and may constitute grounds for review before the Convocation. The Forsaken are our allies in this theatre; treat them accordingly.
III.
Clause of the Primary Mark — Vachon Protocol: The elimination of Captain Aldric Vachon is the supreme objective of this operation. All other Crusade clearance activity is considered subordinate to this imperative. Should any agent locate, engage, or obtain reliable intelligence regarding Vachon's position, they are required to immediately transmit a heliograph signal to Hand-Adept Sunstrike's position and hold until reinforcement arrives. No agent below Knight rank is to engage Vachon alone. Violation of this clause resulting in operative death will be noted without commendation.
IV.
Clause of Evidence — Proof of Kill: For the purposes of operational record-keeping and Convocation accounting, each confirmed Crusader elimination must be evidenced by the recovery of a Scarlet Rank Medallion or tabard fragment, to be delivered to the field Chronicler upon cohort rotation. In the case of Vachon specifically, agents are required to recover his commander's signet ring — distinctive silver with a red-enamel cross — as undeniable proof of execution, given prior false declarations of his death.
V.
Clause of the Light — Conduct in Fel-Adjacent Territory: Tirisfal is a blighted land. Agents are reminded that the ambient shadow corruption may interact unpredictably with Blood Knight techniques of Light-channeling. Extended Holy Wrath expenditure should be tempered with periodic meditation. Any agent exhibiting signs of corruption — disorientation, darkened skin around the eyes, or uncontrolled void resonance — is to be extracted immediately and reported to the Order's healers. We do not lose Knights to the land itself.
VI.
Clause of Alliance — Coordination with the Forsaken: The Undercity's Dark Rangers and Forsaken deathguards maintain parallel operations in Tirisfal. Agents are authorised to share operational intelligence with confirmed Forsaken commanders and may, at their discretion, coordinate strike timing for mutual benefit. However, command authority over this operation remains exclusively with the Blood Knight Order. No Forsaken officer may redirect, countermand, or override the directives contained herein. Courtesy, not subordination, is the operating principle.
VII.
Clause of Secrecy — Operational Silence: This order, its contents, and the existence of a reconstituted Vachon command cell are classified CRIMSON TIER. Agents are forbidden from disclosing the specifics of this mission to non-Order personnel, including Horde faction emissaries, without written dispensation from Hand-Adept Sunstrike. The Scarlet Crusade must not learn the scope of what we know. Loose words in Undercity taverns have ended operations before. Do not let them end this one.
VIII.
Clause of Termination — End-State Conditions: This mandate shall remain active until all three of the following conditions are satisfied: (a) Captain Aldric Vachon is confirmed dead and his signet ring recovered; (b) all identified Crusade encampments in the operational zone have been neutralised; (c) a cohort-wide sweep of all five listed zones produces no new Crusader contact for a period of fourteen consecutive days. Partial completion does not constitute mission success. The operation is complete when the threat is extinguished, not merely reduced.
Penalties for Non-Compliance

The following violations will be brought before the Knight-Lord Convocation for formal censure or greater sanction:

  • Unauthorized parley, mercy-grant, or release of captured Scarlet Crusade members without Knight-Lord dispensation.
  • Breach of operational secrecy resulting in compromise of this order's existence or scope.
  • Unsanctioned solo engagement with Captain Vachon resulting in operative death or incapacitation without prior notification per Clause III.
  • Failure to produce kill evidence per Clause IV upon cohort rotation without a documented field exception.
  • Misidentification of allied Forsaken as Crusaders resulting in friendly-fire incident.
  • Abandonment of the operational theatre without written withdrawal authorisation from a ranking officer.

Agents who distinguish themselves through exceptional service in this operation — and particularly any who deliver proof of Vachon's death — may be considered for commendation before the Convocation and accelerated advancement within the Order's ranks.
