Preamble

Let this order be known to all members of the Blood Knight Order, ranked and auxiliary alike, who draw from the boards at Silvermoon City and the waystation postings across Quel'Thalas: the Wretched have struck against our reconstruction efforts. A supply caravan transporting Ley Restoration Cores — arcane focusing instruments commissioned for use in the ongoing ley-line stabilisation work along the corridor where the Dead Scar once ran — was waylaid on the road northeast of the Goldenmist reconstruction site. The caravan's escort was overwhelmed. The cores were taken. The Wretched who took them have retreated into the Shadowpine Ruins above the Lake Elrendar periphery, where they are almost certainly attempting to drain what they cannot comprehend.

The healing of the Dead Scar was not an ending — it was the beginning of the harder work of rebuilding a land that spent decades dying. Those cores are part of that work. They represent what Quel'Thalas is becoming. Every moment they remain in Wretched hands is a moment that work stalls. The recovery window is narrow. This mandate is posted open because the Order needs it answered now, not after a ranked cohort is assembled and briefed.

Wretched packs operating out of the Shadowpine Ruins have been a known nuisance along this corridor for some time, but their recent aggression suggests organisation beyond opportunistic scavenging. The presence of Siphoner-class Wretched — those who have progressed far enough in their degradation to actively channel and redirect stolen arcane energy — indicates the pack has a functional leadership structure. Where a Siphoner leads, the rank-and-file follow with unusual coordination.

The Ley Restoration Cores will be highly attractive to Siphoners — concentrated ley-attuned arcane energy is precisely what they are built to consume. They will have already begun drawing on the cores' outer resonance fields. Operatives are advised that Siphoners in proximity to active ley instruments will be more volatile than usual and may exhibit accelerated drain capability. Eliminate the Siphoners before attempting to locate the cores. Do not attempt to recover a core while a Siphoner is still standing. They will feel you touch it.

Mission Objectives
Primary Objective
The Siphoner Cull
Locate and eliminate the Wretched Siphoners leading the Shadowpine pack. Intelligence suggests three to five Siphoners are present, likely clustered near the deepest point of the ruins where arcane residue is heaviest. They are not to be driven off. They are not to be allowed to retreat further into the ruins with the essence. They are to be ended.
Secondary Objective
Recovery of the Ley Restoration Cores
Recover all six Ley Restoration Cores taken from the waylaid caravan. The cores are self-sealed and will not have fully degraded if recovered before the Siphoners drain them to inert stone — but that window is closing. A partial recovery of four or more cores is acceptable, but full recovery is the expectation. Count carefully before leaving the ruins.

Shadowpine Pack · Arcane-Drain Capable · Former Sin'dorei
The Siphoners are what remains of Sin'dorei who pursued magic addiction past the point of recovery — stripped of reason, identity, and restraint, retaining only an animal instinct for arcane consumption. They are not salvageable. Whatever they once were is not present in what they have become, and the Order does not permit sentiment to complicate that assessment.

In combat, Siphoners present a specific hazard: their passive drain field will interrupt sustained channelling and may briefly suppress Holy Light output on contact. Operatives are advised to open with Hand of Reckoning to draw them onto consecrated ground before committing to heavier ability expenditure. Let the Light do the work once the field is set. Do not stand at range and trade with them — they are built to punish patience, not aggression.
Blood Knight Combat Doctrine
"A Ranger picks at the edges of a problem. A Blood Knight walks into its centre and hallows the ground beneath her feet." The Wretched are not prey to be stalked — they are corruption to be confronted. An operative who fights from the shadows against the Wretched teaches them nothing and earns nothing. Draw them forward with Hand of Reckoning. Let them come to you. Consecrate the ground before the final blow is dealt so that what falls, falls on sanctified earth. That is not cruelty. That is the correct application of what the Order has built.
Standing Clauses
I.
The Mercy Clause: There is no provision for mercy in this engagement. The Wretched are not prisoners of circumstance awaiting redemption — they are the consequence of will abandoned, and that abandonment is permanent and irreversible at Siphoner stage. An operative who hesitates before a Wretched because they see a Sin'dorei face underneath the degradation is making an emotional error, not a moral one. The Wretched themselves do not hesitate. The Order does not ask its operatives to either. Their termination is a service rendered to the memory of what they once were, and nothing more complicated than that.
II.
The Engagement Clause — Ground and Light: Operatives are expected to engage in the manner befitting the Order's doctrine. Consecrate early. Move forward. The ruins will offer tight corridors and collapsed chambers — use them. Siphoners that are drawn out of their cluster and onto consecrated ground individually are significantly less dangerous than a coordinated pack. Operatives who rely exclusively on ranged harassment will find their Light output degraded over time by proximity to multiple Siphoners. Close the distance and hold it.
III.
The Core Clause — Handling Protocol: The Ley Restoration Cores are to be handled carefully and not tampered with in the field. Each core is an attuned ley-focusing instrument calibrated for specific reconstruction work along the formerly scarred land corridor — they are not weapons, not power sources for personal use, and not curiosities. An operative who feels the core's resonance pulling at their own arcane sensitivity should set it down, step back, and wait for the sensation to pass before picking it up again. The cores respond to need. They were not built for the needs of a single operative in a ruin. Deliver them intact and let the restoration teams do the rest.
IV.
The Delivery Clause — Credit and Protocol: Upon securing the recovered cores, the operative is to proceed directly to Adept Althelor at the Farstrider Retreat and complete the handoff through the designated Blood Knight liaison channel. The cores are not to be handed directly to Farstrider Rangers, regardless of their proximity, their rank, or how reasonable their offer of assistance seems in the moment. The Farstriders did not recover these cores. The Order did. That distinction matters institutionally, and Knight-Lord Bloodvalor has made it clear that the Order's credit for this recovery is not a courtesy — it is a requirement of this mandate's completion.
V.
The Auxiliary Clause — Standing and Recognition: This mandate is open to Auxiliary operatives in full, without restriction on participation or reward eligibility. An Auxiliary who completes both the primary and secondary objectives — confirmed Siphoner eliminations and full six-core recovery — will have their contribution entered into the Field Honour Record under their name, countersigned by the presiding Adept at the Farstrider Retreat. The Order notes those who do the work when the work is inconvenient. That note has a habit of appearing at the right moment.
VI.
The Withdrawal Clause — Tactical Discretion: This mandate carries no requirement to fight to the last. If an operative enters the ruins and finds the pack significantly larger or more organised than intelligence indicated — Siphoner counts above six, signs of external arcane support, structural collapse rendering the deeper chambers inaccessible — they are authorised to withdraw, report their assessment to the nearest Order waystation, and flag the mandate for cohort escalation. A dead Adept recovers none of the essence and eliminates none of the Siphoners. There is no honour in a foolish death, only a vacancy on the board.
Delivery Contact
Adept Althelor Receiving Officer
Blood Knight Liaison · Farstrider Retreat, Eastern Quel'Thalas
Althelor is stationed at the Farstrider Retreat for the duration of this mandate's active window, serving as the Order's on-site liaison. He will receive the recovered cores, conduct a count and integrity assessment, and issue the operative's field completion certificate on the spot. Do not leave the retreat without that certificate — it is the operative's proof of completion for reward and record purposes. Althelor will also log the Siphoner kill count — a brief verbal accounting of numbers is sufficient. He does not need a full account. He needs the cores on the table and the Siphoners confirmed dead.
